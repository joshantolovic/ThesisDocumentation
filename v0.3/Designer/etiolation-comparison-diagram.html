Crowded vs Lonely: Etiolation Mechanics

How neighboring plants change growth shape

🌴 Lonely Plant

0 neighbors within root radius

Neighbor Count: 0
Intensity: 0.0 (0/5)
Length Modifier: 1.0x (normal)
Width Modifier: 1.0x (normal)
Growth Strategy: Short & Wide
Root Spread: Maximum

🌲 Crowded Forest

5+ neighbors per plant

Neighbor Count: 5+
Intensity: 1.0 (5/5)
Length Modifier: 2.0x (double)
Width Modifier: 0.5x (half)
Growth Strategy: Tall & Thin
Root Spread: Limited

👥 How Neighbors Are Detected

Step 1: Check Root Radius

Plant scans all other healthy plants within its current root radius.

rootRadius = 1.0 + (1.0 × widthGrowth)

Step 2: Count Neighbors

Any healthy plant with overlapping root zones counts as a neighbor.

if distance <= rootRadius: neighbor++

Step 3: Calculate Intensity

Intensity increases linearly from 0 to 5 neighbors, then caps at 1.0.

intensity = clamp(neighborCount / 5, 0, 1)

Step 4: Apply Modifiers

Lerp between normal (1.0) and extreme (2.0 or 0.5) based on intensity.

lengthMod = lerp(1.0, 2.0, intensity)
widthMod = lerp(1.0, 0.5, intensity)

🧮 The Etiolation Formula

1
Intensity = neighborCount / 5 (clamped 0-1)
This determines how "crowded" the plant feels. 0 neighbors = 0 intensity, 5+ neighbors = max intensity.
2
lengthModifier = lerp(1.0, etiolationStrength, intensity)
Default etiolationStrength = 2.0, so crowded plants grow 2x taller.
3
widthModifier = lerp(1.0, 1/etiolationStrength, intensity)
Inverse of length modifier. With strength 2.0, crowded plants grow 0.5x wider.

⚖️ Strategic Trade-offs

✓ Benefits of Being Lonely

• Wide growth = larger root radius
• Larger root radius = access to more water tiles
• More water access = consistent growth
• Consistent growth = high competition priority

✗ Costs of Being Lonely

• Short height = less visible biomass
• No etiolation boost = slower vertical growth
• Vulnerable to encroachment = neighbors can crowd in later

✓ Benefits of Being Crowded

• Tall growth = reaching above neighbors for light
• 2x length modifier = rapid vertical growth
• Dense clusters = exclude new competitors

✗ Costs of Being Crowded

• Thin growth = limited root expansion
• Limited roots = fewer water tiles accessible
• Fewer water tiles = lose competition to wider plants
• Losing competition = drought and death
