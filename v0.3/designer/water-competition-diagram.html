‚öîÔ∏è How Competition Works Each Growth Cycle

1

Check Root Radius

Plant scans all soil tiles within its root radius. Root radius grows as the plant's width increases.

rootRadius = 1.0 + (1.0 √ó width growth)

2

Filter by Moisture Range

Only tiles with moisture between 0.3-0.7 are usable. Too dry or too wet = plant can't access it.

Optimal moisture is 0.5 (50%) - furthest from this = slower growth

3

Find Closest Usable Tile

Of all valid tiles, plant wants the closest one (measured from plant base to tile boundary).

Distance = edge-to-edge, not center-to-center

4

Check for Competitors

Is another plant also trying to take water from this tile? Calculate all priority scores.

Priority = vigor √ó (width √ó 2 + length)

5

Winner Takes Water

Highest priority plant extracts moisture (-0.01) from the tile. Losers must find a different tile or face drought.

If no valid tile available = drought timer starts

Soil Tile Colors:

Dry: Moisture < 0.3 (unusable)
Wet: Moisture 0.3-0.7 (usable)
Optimal: Moisture ~0.5 (best growth)
Contested: Multiple plants want it
